CSMC launches latest cancer treatment innovation in PH
  


Cancer continues to be a concern among Filipinos; even more so during the rise in Covid cases brought about by the Omicron variant.

While certain booster vaccines offer to cancer patients stronger protection against the coronavirus, these shots do not mean that cancer treatment should be deprioritized, as cancer patients are still considered a vulnerable population.

Cardinal Santos Medical Center (CSMC)'s Cancer Center aims to find a good balance between Covid and cancer treatment.

CSMC's radiation oncology section dubs the Linac Machine as the "bread and butter" of the unit, as it allows treatment of conventional techniques of radiation treatment as well as the new advanced radiation therapy techniques, namely stereotactic radiation therapy, image-guided radiation therapy and volumetric arc radiation therapy.

Thanks to all these new and advanced techniques, higher radiation doses lethal to the tumor are delivered while minimizing collateral damage to surrounding normal tissues near the tumor, which in turn, will limit toxicities to patients.

Stereotactic radiation therapy is a game changer as an additional armamentarium in battling small tumors and persistent tumors which are situated in areas of the body that are hard to access through surgery and are located in critical structures. With state-of-the-art image guidance and accounting for the movement of mobile organs, CSMC doctors are able to deliver high radiation doses with pinpoint accuracy and precision.
